Provestra Helps You Cope with the Ups-and-Downs of Menopause

As you may know, there are 3 stages of menopause.

These stages include: Perimenopause, Menopause and Postmenopause.

Perimenopause is when your hormones begin changing – preparing your body for menopause.

Menopause is a point in time which happens 12 months after your last period.

When menopause occurs, your ovaries no longer release eggs, your periods cease, and pregnancy is in your rear-view.

But during perimenopause, you can still get pregnant, all while experiencing these common symptoms:

  • Irregular periods
  • Erratic mood swings
  • Migraines
  • Fatigue
  • Urinary incontinence
  • Loss of sex drive
  • Feeling sad
  • Short term memory problems

And did you know… Perimenopause can last 5… 10… even up to 15 years? And you know what else? These symptoms don’t stop after menopause (oh joy!).

You see, after you hit menopause, your body officially transitions into postmenopause.

When postmenopause occurs… the menopausal symptoms listed above continue… and a woman’s body becomes more vulnerable to health threats.
